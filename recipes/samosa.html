A samosa is a fried South Asian and West Asian snack. It is a pastry with a savory filling that mostly consists of vegetables like spiced potatoes, onions, and peas, but can also include meat, fish, or cheese. Its name originates from the Middle Persian word sambosag. It is made in different shapes, including triangular, cone, or crescent, depending on the region. Samosas are often accompanied by chutney, and have origins in medieval times or earlier. Sweet versions are also made. Ingredients:

  • Dough
  • Boiled/Baked Potato
  • Salt
  • Garam Masala
  • Oil (for deep frying)

Steps:

  1. Prepare a dough or buy ready-made ones
  2. Cut dough into small chunks
  3. Add spices and a bit of oil to the potato and mix
  4. Wrap the dough around small portions of the potato mix
  5. Deep fry until golden brown
